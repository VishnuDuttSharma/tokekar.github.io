I am an Assistant Professor in the Bradley Department of Electrical and Computer Engineering at Virginia Tech.

I work in robotics, with a focus on algorithms that enable mobile robots to actively sense and gather data in environments. I also work on developing systems for applications to environmental monitoring. See more about my research here and at my lab's website.

Between October 2014 and July 2015, I was a Post-doctoral researcher at the GRASP lab at University of Pennsylvania, working with Prof. Vijay Kumar.

I completed my Ph.D. in Computer Science from the University of Minnesota in September 2014. My Ph.D. adviser was Prof. Volkan Isler and I was a part of the Robotic Sensor Networks lab. My Ph.D. dissertation was on Placement and Motion Planning Algorithms for Robotic Sensing Systems.

I obtained my Bachelor of Technology degree in Electronics and Telecommunication from College of Engineering, Pune, India. At COEP, I was part of the Robot Study Circle lab.

research

Research Group  ·  Publications  ·  Research Presentation
My research work is in planning for multi-robot systems. I am particularly interested in the paradigm where robots act as mobile sensors --- autonomously exploring new environments and gathering interesting and useful data from the physical world.

Unlike the digital world (where data is often readily available), data collection in the physical world can be dull, dirty, and dangerous. This is where teams of robots acting as mobile, autonomous, data gathering agents can be game-changers. They can collect and relay real-time data from hard-to-reach places, at unprecedented spatio-temporal scales. Access to such data can enable new discoveries and help solve grand challenges in agriculture, oceanography, climate science, surveillance, emergency response, and many such areas.
